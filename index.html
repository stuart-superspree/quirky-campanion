Before you drive

5-second safety check
Pop-top, gas, mirrors, loose items
▾
Before every drive, run through these five. Maple's pop-top is low-profile so easy to forget.
  1. 1Pop-top fully down and latched both sides
  2. 2Gas isolator switch OFF (under hob, red lever)
  3. 3Fridge switched to 12V (auto on VW)
  4. 4Loose items stowed — no pans on hob, no mugs out
  5. 5Mirrors adjusted — Maple is wider than a car
Height: 2.0m. Most car parks and multi-storeys are fine. Watch for 1.9m barriers at supermarkets.
Reversing: rear view is limited — use the mirrors and reverse camera (centre screen auto-switches). Open the sliding door a crack if you need to check a tight corner. Speed & handling: Maple sits high, leans in corners, takes longer to stop. Motorway limit 70 but 60 is comfier.

How Maple works

Heating
Eberspächer diesel heater · 20-sec start
▾
Diesel heater — runs from the fuel tank, no gas needed. Quiet, warm, reliable. Takes about 10 mins to warm the van.
  1. 1Controller is above the sliding door — black unit with dial
  2. 2Press the power button once — light goes green
  3. 3Turn the dial to set temperature (16–24°C)
  4. 4It'll click and whirr as it starts — that's normal
  5. 5Takes ~10 min to warm the van. Hold the button to turn off
60-sec video: Starting the Eberspächer
If it doesn't start: fuel may be low (needs >¼ tank) or battery voltage dropping. Run engine 15 mins and retry.
Troubleshooting ▾
Error code E on display: fuel pump starved. Run engine 15 mins with tank above ¼, then retry.
Runs then cuts out: altitude setting — pop-top down gives better airflow over intake.
Clicking but no heat: glow plug still warming — give it 2 full minutes before worrying.
Water: fresh & grey
40L fresh · filler behind rear door
▾
Maple carries 40L fresh water and 35L grey. Fresh filler is a twist cap behind the rear door on the offside. The pump is automatic — just turn the tap.
  1. 1Fresh filler: open rear door, cap is low down on right side
  2. 2Only use potable water points — blue caps, marked "drinking water"
  3. 3Grey waste empties from tap under the van, offside rear
  4. 4Only empty grey at official disposal points
Winter tip: if it drops below 0°C overnight, drain the system — tap under hob, leave open.
Troubleshooting ▾
Tap runs then stops: usually tank is empty — refill. If not, pump may have airlocked: tap on, wait 30 sec, off, on again.
Pump keeps running: small leak or tap not fully closed. Check under sink and toilet.
Gas hob
Dometic 2-burner · Propane
▾
Two-burner gas hob. Runs off the propane bottle in the front locker. Click-clicks a few times before lighting — that's normal for this unit.
  1. 1Gas isolator ON (under hob, red lever vertical = open)
  2. 2Lift the glass lid fully and latch it up
  3. 3Push burner knob in and turn to ignition (spark)
  4. 4Hold for 5 seconds after flame catches, then release
  5. 5Always turn gas isolator OFF before driving
Jenna says: "Click-click-click three or four times is normal — it's not broken, just shy in the cold."
Troubleshooting ▾
No spark at all: gas isolator might be closed (red lever should be vertical). Also check gas bottle isn't empty.
Lights then goes out: hold the knob in longer — the thermocouple needs 5+ seconds to heat up.
Flame too yellow: burner caps may be wet or misaligned — lift and reseat.
Fridge (12V compressor)
Runs off leisure battery · auto-switches
▾
12V compressor fridge. No gas, no hook-up needed — runs entirely off the leisure battery, topped up by the solar panel. Drains the battery overnight if solar can't keep up (e.g. parked under trees).
  1. 1Dial on top edge controls temperature — 3 is normal
  2. 2Leave it running on the move — it auto-switches to engine power
  3. 3Check battery voltage on the Victron display (green = fine)
Battery getting low? Drive for 30 minutes, or park in direct sun. If under 12.0V, turn the fridge off and drive.
Troubleshooting ▾
Fridge seems warm: check dial hasn't been knocked to 0 — common issue. Set to 3.
Clicking but not cooling: battery under 11.8V — compressor self-protects. Drive or get solar onto it.
Warm food spoiled: if voltage dropped overnight, assume cold chain broken. Bin meat/dairy if in doubt.
Bed conversion
Rock'n'roll below · pop-top above
▾
Two beds: the rock'n'roll seat folds flat into a double downstairs, and the pop-top has a second double upstairs.
  1. 1Downstairs: pull the black strap under the rear seat forward
  2. 2Lift slightly and lower — it'll click into bed position
  3. 3Upstairs: release both roof catches (front corners inside)
  4. 4Push roof up from the centre — it'll rise on struts
  5. 5Ladder stows under the rear seat
Wind warning: don't leave the pop-top up in winds over 40mph. Check the weather card on Home.
Troubleshooting ▾
Rock'n'roll won't click flat: the strap needs a firm pull — weight on the seat helps. If it still won't go, the release catch under the seat may be stuck; lift the cushion and check.
Pop-top struts won't hold: push it right to the top — they click in at full extension only.
Swivel front seats
Both cab seats rotate to face the living area
▾
Driver and passenger seats swivel 180° to face inward — gives you four proper seats around the table when parked up. Handbrake has to be up for the driver seat to clear.
  1. 1Pull the handbrake fully up (driver seat only)
  2. 2Slide the seat fully back on its rails
  3. 3Lift the black lever under the front-left of the seat base
  4. 4Rotate clockwise — it'll click at 90° and 180°
  5. 5Always face forward and lock before driving
Jenna says: "The driver seat is fiddlier than the passenger — lift firmer than you think."
Troubleshooting ▾
Won't rotate: steering wheel might be in the way — turn it fully right first.
Won't click back to driving position: it needs to be dead straight. Rock it gently until the click.
Solar & leisure battery
100W solar · 110Ah AGM · Victron monitor
▾
Maple is off-grid capable — solar charges the leisure battery, which runs the fridge, lights, USB and water pump. No hook-up needed for typical use.
  1. 1Victron BMV-712 display is above the sliding door
  2. 2Green = healthy (12.6V+) · Orange = getting low · Red = charge now
  3. 3Park in sun where possible — even cloudy UK days help
  4. 4Running engine for 30 mins adds significant charge
No hook-up cable needed. Maple doesn't take mains. If you're on a site, just don't connect anything.
Troubleshooting ▾
Victron showing red: below 12.0V. Drive 30 mins minimum or move to sun.
Solar not charging on sunny day: something may be shading the panel — check for leaves or a bag on the roof.
USB / lights flickering: voltage low. Switch off non-essentials and drive.
Porta-potti (under seat)
Thetford 365 · stows below rear seat
▾
Portable flush toilet for overnight emergencies. Slides out from under the rock'n'roll seat. Empty at any campsite chemical disposal point (or Elsan point).
  1. 1Lift the rear seat base, slide Porta-Potti forward
  2. 2Use normally — pump flush handle on top
  3. 3Empty at any chemical disposal point (site office will direct)
  4. 4Blue toilet fluid supplied in the cupboard — add after emptying
Never empty into a regular toilet or a grey waste point. Only chemical/Elsan points.
Troubleshooting ▾
Flush won't pump: top tank is empty — refill with fresh water and a cap of blue fluid.
Smell: add more blue fluid, and empty sooner than you think — it builds up fast in warm weather.
Windows & vents
Sliding windows, roof vent, condensation control
▾
Maple has sliding windows on both sides and a Heki roof vent. A cracked vent overnight is the single best thing you can do against condensation.
  1. 1Side windows: pinch the two grey tabs together, slide across
  2. 2Fly screens pull across from the forward edge — don't force
  3. 3Roof Heki: twist the centre knob a half-turn, push up from the middle
  4. 4Pull-down blind under the Heki gives you blackout
Condensation: leave the Heki cracked on the first notch overnight, even in winter. 10 min airing after cooking makes a massive difference.
Wind-out awning (Fiamma F45)
2.5m canopy on the sliding-door side
▾
Handle-wound awning on the offside. Gives you about 2.5m of shelter — brilliant for cooking in rain. Can be damaged quickly by wind, so never leave it out unattended.
  1. 1Winding handle lives in the under-bed storage — the bent silver rod
  2. 2Hook it into the awning front bar, turn to wind out
  3. 3Support legs fold down from the front bar — lock them at the right height
  4. 4Peg the front legs down in anything but still conditions
  5. 5Wind back in reverse — never drive with it out
Wind warning: retract it if wind picks up over 20mph. A sudden gust will rip it off and it's a £600+ repair.
Troubleshooting ▾
Legs won't lock at height: the knurled collars need twisting firm — they're stiff.
Won't wind back in smoothly: usually someone leaning on it — clear the space under.
Tailgate bike rack (added)
Thule Elite · fits 2 adult bikes
▾
Bike rack is fitted on pickup if you've added it as an extra. Sits on the tailgate — you can still open the tailgate with bikes loaded, just slowly.
  1. 1Bikes go on frame-first — loop the strap round the downtube, click buckle
  2. 2Second bike faces the opposite way (handlebars clear the first)
  3. 3Wheel straps secure both wheels — no movement allowed
  4. 4Add the supplementary number-plate repeater (supplied) — legal requirement
  5. 5Check bolts after first 20 miles and each morning
Reverse camera will be blocked with bikes on — mirrors only, go slow.
Locking up & overnight
Sliding door quirk · what to do wild-camping
▾
Standard VW key — all doors lock from the fob. The sliding door sometimes needs a firm pull to fully catch before the lock works.
  1. 1Close sliding door firmly — listen for the second click
  2. 2Press fob lock — all four indicators flash once
  3. 3Check all windows closed (roof Heki too) before leaving
  4. 4Overnight: lock from inside by pulling the slider closed, sill catch down
  5. 5Spare key is not provided — don't lose the fob
Wild-camping: park with the sliding door away from any road. Don't leave valuables visible. In towns, prefer paid car parks overnight — rarely any cheaper than a small campsite anyway.

Van quirks & quick tips
  • Gas hob clicks 3–4 times before lighting, especially in the cold. Totally normal.
  • The rear sliding door sticks a bit in damp weather — firm lift and slide, don't force.
  • There's a hidden Bluetooth speaker in the overhead locker. Code 0000.
  • If the fridge seems warm, check the dial hasn't been knocked to zero — happens often.
Jenna's favourite campsites
  • Burrowhayes Farm, Porlock — family-run, dog-friendly, river at the back. My first choice on Exmoor.
  • Troytown Farm, Isles of Scilly — if you can get there. Cows, cliffs, sunsets. Bring Maple on the ferry.
  • Porlock Weir car park (free overnight) — stunning at sunset. Polite, quiet, no facilities.
  • Lower Hyde, Ninebanks — Northumberland hideaway, proper off-grid feel, dark skies.
Things people often miss
  • The curtain hooks — full blackout is a revelation, especially in June when it's light till 10.
  • The outdoor shower tap, rear-offside. Amazing after a beach day.
  • The little map drawer under the passenger seat — I've left a couple of favourites in there.

Key policies · plain English

Security deposit

▾

A £500 hold is placed on your card at pickup. It's not charged — just reserved. Released within 7 days of return if all is well.

Any damage or missing items may be deducted from this deposit (up to £1,000 excess).

Damage & insurance

▾

Comprehensive insurance is included. Your liability is capped at £1,000 excess unless you've added the excess reduction extra.

Report any damage through the Help tab — even small things. Photos at pickup and return protect everyone.

Cleaning & fuel on return

▾

Return Maple clean inside — surfaces wiped, floor swept, no food in the fridge, waste tank empty.

Return with a full diesel tank. Dirty interiors incur a £75 valet fee; missing fuel is refuelled at cost + £25.

Pets (you have 1 dog booked)

▾

Dogs are welcome in Maple. Please hoover thoroughly and give soft furnishings a brush before return. A £25 pet-clean fee applies if extra cleaning is needed.

Never leave dogs alone in a hot van.

Late return

▾

Need an extra hour? Contact Jenna before your return time. Subject to availability.

Unplanned late return: £25/hour. After 3 hours late, full day rate.

Europe travel & VOH certificate

▾

Your current booking is UK-only. To take Maple to Europe you need a VOH (Vehicle On Hire) certificate plus the Europe Kit add-on (GB plate, headlamp beam deflectors, UKCA high-vis, breathalyser where required).

VOH takes 2–3 working days to process. Ask Quirky support before you plan a Euro trip.
